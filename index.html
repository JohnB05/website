Dust Veil

During the Dustveil mission, the blood of the energy-charged life forms of K807B was to be collected and processed at bases. But a massive alien attack overran almost all of the bases, leaving only chaos in its wake - one remained standing, damaged but still functional. As a genetically modified soldier, you fight alone or in squads to secure this last bastion. The lead organization is sending reinforcements, but until then, the motto is: hang in there! The concentrated blood obtained with the help of the S.H.R.E.D.D.E.R. is pumped into the circulatory systems of the tech soldiers to supply them with energy. They search for and eliminate enemies on their own or, on command, combine their energy for devastating explosions. Your own body can also be enhanced by the alien power source. Short, deadly sprints of superhuman strength that hurl you through enemies.

Bachelor

This project is my bachelor’s thesis on the topic of the effectiveness and transferability of reward systems. It is tested through two experiments that vary the number and type of rewards between tests in order to determine how players feel under different reward conditions. The game is a 2D platformer in which the player can shoot enemies with a weapon to fill an XP bar and unlock new weapons. Along the way, there are checkpoints, coins, and other intrinsic rewards that the player encounters during gameplay.

Kaktus Hexe

A small tutorial for a bigger idea. Despite the warning of the town folks you go into the mystical wood Skadi. A storm surprises you and you get lost. Between the tall trees you discover a small house. You decide to go inside for shelter. In this game you have to decide if you want to defeat the creatures you meet or if you want to make friends.

Good morning Star

This game is a mix of 2D and 3D — a hack-and-slash adventure in which the player fights their way through a dungeon using a morning star. The morning star can also be used as a grappling hook or a swing rope.

About me

Hi, I’m John Burger, a Master’s student in Game Engineering and Visual Computing. I’m passionate about creating interactive worlds and exploring how technology and creativity can come together to tell powerful stories. My main tools include Unreal Engine, Unity, Blender and C++, and I love experimenting with new techniques to bring ideas to life, from gameplay mechanics to visual effects. Outside of development, I enjoy video editing and image design for Twitch and YouTube, as well as spending time at the gym to stay active and balanced. If you’d like to connect, collaborate, or just chat about games and tech, feel free to get in touch!
